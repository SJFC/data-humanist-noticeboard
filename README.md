# data-humanist-noticeboard
# You Are Not a Bar Chart
### An inquiry around how we represent lives in data

---

This project is part of MDes study at the **University of Sunderland**, within a module exploring design for social impact. It asks whether there are more human-centred ways of representing individual experience alongside — not instead of — conventional institutional data.

## What is data humanism?

Data humanism is a practice that looks beyond the graphs, dashboards and bar charts of big data to the individual human stories that sit behind them. It asks what is lost when a life becomes a number, and what we might gain by representing data differently. The term is associated with the work of information designer Giorgia Lupi, whose *Dear Data* project and subsequent writing argue for data practices that foreground subjectivity, emotion and lived experience rather than erasing them.

## The project

This website is the digital companion to a physical noticeboard installation. The noticeboard — made from cut-out Victorian natural history imagery, handwritten notes and masking tape — is placed in a corridor space within a higher education institution, where students encounter it by chance and can choose to engage with it or not.

Together, the physical board and this site aim to create a low-stakes, informal and genuinely opt-in way for individuals to have a say in how they are represented. Rather than being sorted into institutional categories — tick-box identities, demographic fields, protected characteristics — a visitor is invited to choose a specimen: a feather, a snail, a mushroom, a shell. Or something else entirely. They can add a note explaining why, in their own words, or leave it without comment.

The cabinet of responses that accumulates over time becomes a collective, human-centred record: an alternative to the pie chart that sits alongside it rather than replacing it.

## Why Victorian natural history?

The visual language of Victorian specimen plates carries the *form* of taxonomy — ordered, labelled, authoritative — while being entirely empty of the categories institutional data uses to sort people. A feather has no socioeconomic status. A snail has no protected characteristics. Choosing one as a self-representation means using the structure of classification while refusing its logic. The colonial history embedded in that visual tradition is not incidental; it is part of what the project is thinking with.

## Status

This is a design prototype, developed as a speculative intervention. The cabinet of specimens shown here is seeded with illustrative responses for portfolio demonstration purposes. A live deployment with persistent shared storage would require a backend service such as Supabase or Firebase.
